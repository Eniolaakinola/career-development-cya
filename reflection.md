# Reflection

## Activity Description

For this Career Development CYA, I chose to apply to software
engineering internships targeting Summer 2026. The expectation was
to invest approximately 11.5 hours in a career-aligned activity and
produce a verifiable deliverable. I researched 7 companies ranging
from local Pacific Northwest employers like SEL, BECU, and Avista
to large tech companies like Amazon, Cloudflare, and Intel. For each
company I wrote a tailored cover letter, researched the role and
organization, and submitted a complete application with my resume.

## Technical and Professional Decisions

The most important decision I made was to prioritize quality over
quantity. Rather than mass-applying to dozens of roles, I selected
7 companies that each had a clear connection to my skills and goals,
and I wrote a distinct cover letter for each one. For example, I led
with my microcontroller project for hardware-oriented companies like
SEL and Intel, and led with my C# spreadsheet application for
roles like BECU and Harder Mechanical.

I also deliberately chose a mix of reach companies (Amazon, Cloudflare,
Intel) and high-probability targets (SEL, BECU, Avista, Harder
Mechanical). This was a strategic decision to maximize both my chance
of landing something and my exposure to top-tier engineering culture.

## Contributions

This was an independent effort. I had previously researched internships on LinkedIn and saved them for later. 
I also applied to some a while ago and haven't received a response yet
I conducted all company research,
wrote every cover letter from scratch, tailored to each role, managed
My own application timeline, and documented everything in this
repository.

## Quality Assessment

My strongest applications were SEL and Intel because my embedded
systems project maps directly to their work. My weakest was probably
Amazon I could have spent more time preparing for their technical
screen. If I repeated this, I would have to start earlier in the semester
and add LinkedIn outreach to employees at each company before
submitting

## Career Alignment

Applying to these internships directly advances my short-term goal
of landing a Summer 2026 role, but the process itself built skills
I will use for years: articulating my technical background clearly,
researching organizations strategically, and presenting myself
professionally in writing. Each company I targeted works in systems,
infrastructure, or enterprise software  the areas I want to build
a career in. Even applications that don't result in offers have
moved me forward, because I now have polished materials I can reuse
and a clearer sense of how to position myself in a competitive market.
